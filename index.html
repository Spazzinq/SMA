WHAT IS IT?

According to AddictionCenter, a clinically-reviewed guide to different types of addiction, social media addiction (SMA) is a behavioral addiction characterized by “excessive or compulsive use” of social networking sites and applications. Similar to substance use disorders, SMA symptoms include mood modification, salience, tolerance, withdrawal symptoms, conflict, and relapse. Psychologists estimate that 16 to 33 million Americans meet the criteria for SMA today (Hilliard).

HELP YOURSELF

The best advice may not be what you want to hear, but according to the Center for Humane Technology, you should turn off social media application and website notifications (at least those that aren’t direct messages), and uninstall apps that “profit off of addiction, distraction, outrage, polarization, and misinformation” like Facebook, Tiktok, Snapchat, and Instagram. But for those who want to take it one step at a time, you can download tools to change your habits, reduce distractions, and stay focused on your goals. Finally, set boundaries for yourself: don’t use social media in the mornings, when you eat, or when you go to bed (“Take Control”).
